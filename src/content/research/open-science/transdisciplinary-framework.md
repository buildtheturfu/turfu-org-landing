# TURFU A.R.T. — Transdisciplinary Framework

> Action / Research / Tiers (the included middle) — the meta-framework under which this research programme operates.

---

## What TURFU A.R.T. means

**TURFU** = Centre Transdisciplinaire de Recherche (Transdisciplinary Research Center), founded by Christopher Keo, Strasbourg, France.

**A.R.T.** is the operational triptych:

- **A** — Action : research is grounded in concrete intervention. Theoretical work serves a purpose beyond explanation.
- **R** — Research : theoretical depth is preserved. Rigour is non-negotiable.
- **T** — Tiers : the *included middle* in the sense of Lupasco-Nicolescu transdisciplinarity. Knowledge is produced at the traversal of levels of reality, not by reduction.

Important: **T is NOT "Transparence" or "Transdisciplinarité"**. The letter signifies *the third* — the structural middle term that emerges only when the framework operates across levels.

---

## The transdisciplinary lineage

The framework draws on:

- **Stéphane Lupasco** (1900-1988) — logician of dynamic energy, formulator of the *logic of the included middle* (logique du tiers inclus)
- **Basarab Nicolescu** (b. 1942) — physicist and philosopher; foundational text *La Transdisciplinarité — Manifeste* (1996) defines transdisciplinarity as the cross-cutting of levels of reality
- **Edgar Morin** (1921-2024) — complex thought, recursive epistemology

In this programme's papers, the lineage is acknowledged explicitly (Paper 1 cites Nicolescu 1996; Paper 2 returns to the traversal-of-levels argument in §4.4).

## How A.R.T. shapes the research programme

### Level-traversal as the unit of work

Both published papers operate by identifying a **structural pattern** that recurs across multiple levels of reality, without reducing one level to another:

- **Paper 1 (BioSystems)**: the *viability sketch* ThP is instantiated biologically via Rosen's (M,R)-systems, and is proposed as a structural template for any self-organising system
- **Paper 2 (SRBS)**: the same structural primitives (P-system, admissible morphism, exit) are read at the level of multi-level cooperative systems — cancer, totalitarianism, ecological invasion as three independent structural realizations of one diagnostic pattern

The cross-paper continuity is itself an A.R.T. signature: the formal substrate is preserved across levels of application.

### Action: the third level of the programme

Beyond formal and theoretical work, TURFU is structured to deploy its insights operationally. Examples (current programme):
- NLEX (Natural Law Explorer) — public platform for AI-mediated debates
- Bane Lao 2.0 — bilingual project-management platform for a family restaurant relocation (Lao/French)
- Future: governance design tooling derived from the viability framework

The Action dimension is what distinguishes TURFU from a pure academic-philosophical programme.

### Tiers: methodological consequence

The included-middle commitment has concrete methodological consequences:

1. **No reductionism** — the political case is not reduced to a biological metaphor. The cancer/totalitarianism connection is presented as a *shared structural pattern*, not as a substantive isomorphism.
2. **No solipsism** — the framework does not claim to be the only valid reading. Paper 2 §5.3 explicitly presents 4 normative completions (property/non-aggression, Ostromian, Habermasian, Pettit) as equally compatible with the structural scaffolding.
3. **No premature closure** — the formal apparatus is presented as a *sketch* (in the technical sense of categorical theory): a generalised, restricted formal frame that admits domain-specific instantiations without being identical to any one of them.

## Why this matters for Open Science

The transdisciplinary frame disciplines two failure modes common in interdisciplinary work:

**Failure mode 1 — the "metaphor mistake"**: treating an analogy as a substantive claim. ("Cancer is like totalitarianism" → "we have explained totalitarianism via cancer".)

A.R.T. corrects this by distinguishing structural pattern (preserved across levels) from substantive isomorphism (not claimed).

**Failure mode 2 — the "reduction mistake"**: claiming that one level can substitute for another. (Reducing political phenomena to biological mechanisms; or vice versa.)

A.R.T. corrects this by treating each level as having its own primitive predicates of admissibility, exit, and endogeneity. The same structural pattern manifests at different levels without any level being reducible to another.

These corrections are operationalised in Paper 2 §4 ("Structural pattern, not surface analogy") and in §6.2 ("Limits of the current formalism").

## The TURFU A.R.T. lexicon

For external collaborators or readers new to the framework:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Sketch** | A generalised categorical specification with a formal underlying graph, cones, and avoidance clauses (Paper 1, §2) — not an informal "outline" |
| **P-system** | A state object S, a functional membrane ∂S ⊆ S, and a self-production endomorphism φ : S → S that must remain endogenous (Paper 1, §3) |
| **Admissible morphism** | An inter-system interaction that preserves the endogeneity of φ at each level (Paper 1, Def. A.1) |
| **Monopolistic predation** | A non-admissible morphism for which either no competing interaction or no admissible exit morphism is available — or both (Paper 1, Def. A.2; Paper 2, §3) |
| **Relational exit** | The exit morphism does not require spatial displacement but only cessation of the predatory relationship (Paper 2, §5) |
| **Structural realization** | Domain-specific instantiation of an abstract structural pattern, preserving the pattern under the domain's own primitive predicates (Paper 2, §4) |

These are not jargon. They are the operational vocabulary the framework produces. Each is anchored in a precise formal definition in the published Paper 1 (BioSystems, 2026).
