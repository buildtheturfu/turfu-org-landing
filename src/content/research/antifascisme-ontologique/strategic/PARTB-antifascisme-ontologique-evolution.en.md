## Submission Strategy

**Sequential, not parallel** (Wiley is explicit on duplicate/redundant publication)

1. **SRBS first** (B1) — Wiley prestige, IF 1.8, ~15% acceptance
2. **If rejected → Cosmos and History** (B2) — immediately, not a day lost

### Journal Profiles

**SRBS (Wiley)**
- Scope: general systems theory, cybernetics, autopoiesis, complexity science, viable approaches, governance
- Format: preprints accepted, AI disclosure (not prohibition)
- Review: standard peer review

**Cosmos and History**
- Scope: natural and social philosophy, transdisciplinary
- Format: 4,000-8,000 words typical, double-blind
- References: **footnotes** as the main format (B2 to convert from author-date)
- Open access

**Constructivist Foundations — DISCARDED**
- Excellent intellectual fit BUT major procedural friction
- Forbids generative AI in writing
- Forbids public preprints/drafts during evaluation
- Incompatible with TURFu workflow (Opus co-elaboration, NLEX transparency, public versioning)

## Gemini 3 Review — External evaluation B1 SRBS

Gemini 3 was asked to evaluate the B1 submission and make its stakes explicit. Here is the full analysis.

### I. The Stake: From Morality to Topology

The shift from version A (NLEX) to B1 (SRBS) radically changes the nature of the exchange. The objective is no longer to convince that "totalitarianism is evil", but to demonstrate that it is **structurally non-viable** in the same way as a cancer.

- **Formal identity**: The text refuses analogy ("totalitarianism resembles cancer") in favour of a **structural identity**. According to the ThP categorical sketch, both are instantiations of **monopolistic predation**.
- **SRBS target**: The choice of this journal is surgical — its readers already command autopoiesis and the closure of efficient causation (Rosen). The paper can enter directly into the heart of the matter: how an inclusion between levels (cell/tissue or citizen/state) becomes pathological.

### II. The Objective: Making the "Capture Pathology" Intelligible

The paper sets a universal diagnostic criterion based on category theory.

**Monopolistic Predation (The Disease):**
- A system enters the pathological phase when it becomes a "topological prison".
- Structure: A morphism f: A → B where neither competition (g: A' → B) nor an exit morphism (e: B → C) exists.
- Effect: The self-production function φ of the sub-system becomes **heteronomous** — it no longer produces its own organisation, it is "produced" and consumed by the higher level.

**Relational Exit (The Remedy) — major contribution:**
- Exit is not necessarily spatial (relocating); it is **relational** (ceasing the capture relation).
- Level 1: Emigration (emergency exit)
- Level 2: Disaffiliation (legal pluralism)
- Level 3: *In situ* dissociation (sovereign determination without displacement)

### III. Implications: A New Governance Design

The direct implication: the viability of a social architecture does not depend on its perceived "goodness", but on its **structural porosity**.

| Classical Concept | Viability Diagnostic (ThP) | Design Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Democracy (Voice) | Insufficient if exit is blocked | The vote does not protect against monopolistic predation |
| State Sovereignty | Potentially pathological (territorial monopoly) | Need to guarantee admissible exit morphisms |
| Liberty / Autonomy | Endogeneity of φ | Maintain the sub-system's capacity to produce itself |

**DAO/Digital case:** Decentralised architectures (protocol forks, algorithmic asset withdrawal as in Moloch DAO) are the first concrete implementations of **social apoptosis**. Conversely, a platform that locks in user data (No-Exit) is diagnosed as a structural "pre-cancer".

### IV. Gemini Final Evaluation

**Identified risk point:** Proposition 2 (multi-level compatibility) is close to a tautology if the social agent is not more rigorously defined. However, shifting the diagnosis from "the object" to "the relation between levels" is a major epistemological advance.

> **Gemini 3 final diagnosis:** "The paper succeeds in transforming radical libertarianism into a **requirement of cybernetic health**."

**Note:** Gemini proposes deepening Open Questions Q1-Q4 to prepare responses to future SRBS referees.
