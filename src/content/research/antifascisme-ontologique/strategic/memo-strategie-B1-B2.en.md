# TURFU team internal note — B1 / B2 Strategy

We now have **two academic versions derived from the same argumentative core**.
They share about **85% of the content**, but differ in their **rhetorical mask**, their **entry point**, their **philosophical temperature**, and above all their **target journal**.

The idea is not to produce two competing papers, but **the same paper prepared for two different editorial doors**, with **only one active submission at a time**.

---

## 1) Version B1 — systemic / governance mask

### Title

**Relational Exit as a Structural Condition of Viability in Multi-Level Cooperative Systems**

### Target

**Systems Research and Behavioral Science (SRBS)**

### Why this journal

SRBS presents itself as a **transdisciplinary journal of systems science**, covering **systems approaches in all disciplines**. Its overview emphasises **holistic understanding** and **purposeful design** of complex human, ecological and social systems, and the announced scope explicitly includes **general systems theory, cybernetics, autopoiesis, complexity science** and adjacent approaches.

In other words, it is a journal where our basic vocabulary does not need to be justified from scratch:

* viability,
* multi-level cooperation,
* autopoiesis,
* admissible morphisms,
* governance design,
* structural pathology.

The fit is reinforced by the fact that the journal also publishes recent work on topics close to ours, for example:

* the conceptualisation of **technology as an autopoietic system**;
* papers on **democracy** and the freedom of scientific inquiry;
* work in **organisational cybernetics** around viable systems.

### Positioning of B1

B1 is the most **disciplined** version, the most **immediately submittable**, and the closest to the language expected by a systems-science journal.

It enters via:

* the **viability of multi-level cooperative systems**,
* the distinction between **distributed** and **monopolistic predation**,
* then makes **relational exit** emerge as a **design principle** for governance.

In B1:

* ThP is in the foreground;
* the political dimension arrives as a **systemic application**;
* convergence with property / NAP remains **cautious and late**;
* there is no "ontological antifascism" up front.

### Strategic role

B1 is our **first shot**.
It is the best vehicle to test the most robust and academically acceptable version of the programme, without over-exposing the most explosive parts.

## 2) Version B2 — philosophical mask / political viability

### Title

**Relational Exit and the Structural Conditions of Political Viability**

### Target

**Cosmos and History: The Journal of Natural and Social Philosophy**

### Why this journal

Cosmos and History presents itself as a **peer-reviewed**, **open access** journal of **natural and social philosophy**, explicitly open to contributions from thinkers across **all disciplines** as long as they have a genuine philosophical ambition. The journal operates **double blind review** and considers **4,000 to 8,000 words** as the typical range for an article.

It is therefore a very natural target for a version that is:

* more explicitly philosophical,
* more transdisciplinary,
* more ambitious in its political reading,
* while remaining disciplined.

### Positioning of B2

B2 is the most **intellectually distinctive** version.

It enters via the political question:

* what makes **political pathology** possible?
* what are the **structural conditions** of non-pathology?
* why is exit not a simple liberal detail, but a deep condition of viability?

In B2:

* ThP is a **formal support** rather than an entry point;
* the central contribution is **relational exit**;
* the transdisciplinary dimension is more visible;
* the dialogue **Lupasco / Santacreu / Proudhon** can exist;
* **ontological antifascism** appears only in the conclusion, as a philosophical consequence, not as an initial banner.

### Strategic role

B2 is our **editorial plan B**, but also probably our **best philosophical signature**.

If B1 is the paper easiest to send first, B2 is the one that most clearly expresses the conceptual singularity of TURFU.

---

## 3) Real difference between the two versions

### What is shared

The two versions share the core:

* multi-level viability,
* distributed vs monopolistic predation,
* three instantiations,
* relational exit,
* methodological note: demonstrated / instantiated / proposed / programmatic,
* the four open questions.

### What changes

What changes is not the main substance, but:

* the **title**,
* the **abstract**,
* the **introduction**,
* the **temperature of the conclusion**,
* the **surface vocabulary**,
* and the way of presenting the political scope.

In practice:

* **B1** = systems / governance / viability reading
* **B2** = philosophy / political viability / structural pathology reading

---

## 4) Strategy adopted

### Submission order

1. **B1 → SRBS**
2. if rejected, **B2 → Cosmos and History**

### Why this order

Wiley indicates that **preprints** are in principle compatible with submission, while reminding authors to avoid any **redundant publication** and to treat a submission as a single manuscript under evaluation.
For its part, Cosmos and History specifies that a submitted text must not already be **published** or **simultaneously under evaluation elsewhere**.

So the rule of method is simple:

> **only one active submission at a time**

This is not dispersion.
It is a strategy of **clean sequentiality**:

* one first attempts the strongest systemic journal,
* then, if scope or editorial-line rejection occurs, one switches immediately to the most natural philosophical journal.
