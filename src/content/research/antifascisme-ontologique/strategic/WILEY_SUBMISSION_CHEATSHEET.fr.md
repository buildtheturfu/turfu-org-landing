## ÉTAPE 4 — Abstract (copier-coller)

```
This paper develops a formal account of the structural conditions under which multi-level cooperative systems remain viable or become pathological. Drawing on the viability sketch (Keo, 2026, BioSystems, 265, 105811), it defines monopolistic predation as a non-admissible morphism lacking either an available competitor or an admissible exit morphism (or both). Cancer, political totalitarianism, and ecological invasion on captive habitat are presented as three independent structural realizations of the same pattern at different levels of reality. The central contribution is the concept of relational exit: the exit morphism does not require spatial displacement but only cessation of the predatory relationship, opening three hierarchical levels (emergency emigration, institutional disaffiliation, in situ dissociation). The paper derives two structural principles — a non-heteronomization principle and a structural necessity of admissible exit — and shows convergence with governance design principles centred on protected space, boundary integrity, and mutual recognition. This is a formal diagnostic framework, not a complete political theory.
```
