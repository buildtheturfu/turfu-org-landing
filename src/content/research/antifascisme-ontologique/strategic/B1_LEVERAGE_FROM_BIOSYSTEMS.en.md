# B1 Leverage from BioSystems Acceptance — Strategic Memo

**Question:** how can the BioSystems acceptance (BIOSYS-D-26-00374R1, 2026-05-06) + reviewer feedback + the requested references strengthen the B1 submission to SRBS, without overloading B1 or diverting it from its own contribution.

**Guiding principle** (consistent with Opus + ICMJE/COPE): the derivation is legitimate iff B1 has its own distinct contribution. BioSystems = formal substrate cited, not duplication.

---

| Asset | Source | Where to use in B1 | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Formal acceptance** | Editorial decision, 2026-05-06 | SRBS cover letter (opening paragraph) | 1× cover letter |
| **R3 verdict** *("new important insights into the foundations of theoretical biology")* | Reviewer 3 (added by editor at R&R stage) | SRBS cover letter, validation paragraph | 1× cover letter, **never in the manuscript** |

| **Reference forthcoming** *(Keo 2026, BioSystems)* | The accepted paper itself | B1 manuscript: intro + systemic bridge + bibliography | 2-3× manuscript |
| **Methodological lineage**: Rosetta Stone approach (Baez & Stay 2011) | §1.2 BioSystems paper | B1 manuscript: if B1 does translation work, cite as a legitimised method | 1× manuscript |
| **Endogeneity-as-non-factorisation** *(notion of K(S,E))* | §3.3 + §3.6 BioSystems paper | B1 manuscript: do **not** re-import the machinery; mention as "structural background" | 1× footnote |

**Golden rule:** asymmetry cover letter ↔ manuscript.
- **Cover letter** = privileged place to assert legitimacy (acceptance + verdicts) — it is private, read only by the SRBS editor.
- **Manuscript** = place where one cites the source paper soberly as substrate. No reviewer quotes, no "as Reviewer 3 of BioSystems noted...". That would come across as unprofessional self-promotion and would make an SRBS reviewer wince.

## 2. Mechanics of the SRBS cover letter

Suggested structure (4 paragraphs, ~350-450 words).

### ¶1 — The submission itself

```
Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit for consideration "Relational Exit as a Structural
Condition of Viability in Multi-Level Cooperative Systems" for publication
in Systems Research and Behavioral Science. The manuscript [N words]
develops a systems-theoretic framework in which exit is reconstructed as a
structural condition for the viability of complex systems composed of
self-organizing agents.
```

### ¶2 — The distinct contribution

```
The paper makes three contributions: (i) it introduces relational exit as
a category distinct from market exit, formal exit, or migration; (ii) it
reconstructs consent as a systems property requiring maintained boundary
control rather than a single act of authorization; (iii) it interprets
private property, non-aggression, and panarchic institutional pluralism
as functional analogues of membrane, non-destructive coupling, and
multi-affiliation in self-organizing systems. The framework is
illustrated through three historical cases of exit suppression: the
Reichsfluchtsteuer (Bajohr 2002; Ritschl 2019), the Chinese hukou system
(Chan & Zhang 1999), and the 1994 Cuban Balsero crisis (Hoffmann 2010).
```

### ¶3 — The research programme (transparency declaration)

```
This manuscript is part of a broader research programme on structural
viability and non-captive governance. It is conceptually related to a
separate formal paper, "A Categorical Sketch for Viability: Formalizing
the Structural Invariants of Self-Organizing Systems" (Keo, forthcoming
in BioSystems, BIOSYS-D-26-00374R1, accepted 2026-05-06), which develops
the mathematical model referenced briefly in §[X]. The present article
does not reproduce that formal model; rather, it develops a distinct
systems-theoretic argument applicable to social, institutional, and
governance contexts. Two further articles in this programme are planned,
targeting AI & Society / Ethics and Information Technology and Journal
of Institutional Economics / Constitutional Political Economy
respectively, with non-overlapping contributions.
```

### ¶4 — BioSystems validation (careful deployment)

```
The forthcoming BioSystems paper passed peer review in a single round of
revision. Reviewer 3, added by the editor during revision, characterized
the formal model as providing "new important insights into the
foundations of theoretical biology." I mention this only to indicate
that the formal substrate underlying the present systems-theoretic
argument has been independently validated; the contribution evaluated
here is distinct and stands or falls on its own merits.
```

### ¶5 — Closing

```
The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under
consideration by another journal. AI tools (Claude Opus, GPT, Gemini)
were used for language editing, organization of revision materials, and
clarity checks; final wording, argumentation, and decisions are mine.
This is disclosed in the manuscript per Wiley editorial policy.

Suggested reviewers: [3-5 names — see §4 below].

Sincerely,
Christopher Keo
TURFu — Centre Transdisciplinaire de Recherche, Strasbourg, France
ORCID: 0009-0000-8290-1416
```

**Why this structure:**
- ¶3 (transparency) **before** ¶4 (validation): one declares the research programme, *then* validates via BioSystems. Reversing the order would give the impression of hiding the triptych behind the acceptance.
- ¶4 uses the R3 verdict but **immediately** defuses it with "the contribution evaluated here is distinct and stands or falls on its own merits." This neutralises the objection "you are trying to ride on BioSystems."
- Do not name Igamberdiev personally — he is the BioSystems editor but SRBS does not know him, and naming him would look like name-dropping.

## 5. Anti-attack armour (pre-empting SRBS via BioSystems lessons)

The BioSystems R&R revealed 4 recurring lines of attack. B1 will likely meet them in adapted form. Pre-emption:

### 5.1 "Biological reductionism / metaphor abuse" attack
**BioSystems form**: R2 questioned whether social autopoiesis (Luhmann) was serious or hand-waving (R2.9).
**Likely SRBS form**: "You illegitimately transpose biological concepts (membrane, autopoiesis) to the political."
**B1 pre-emption**: add in §1 an explicit note:
> *"The framework borrows vocabulary from autopoiesis and self-organization, but the argument does not depend on a literal isomorphism between biological and social systems. The biological theory provides a conceptual lexicon for relational structures whose formal substrate is developed elsewhere (Keo, forthcoming); the present paper deploys this lexicon for institutional and governance analysis without claiming that biology proves the politics."*

### 5.2 "You are reinventing Hirschman" attack
**BioSystems form**: NA (formal framework).
**Likely SRBS form**: "Your categories are already in Hirschman 1970, you bring nothing new."
**B1 pre-emption**: cite **Hirschman 1970** + **Hoffmann 2010** explicitly, and clarify the delta:
> *"Hirschman's exit/voice/loyalty framework, as reactivated by Hoffmann (2010), treats exit as a discrete option. Relational exit, by contrast, is a continuous structural property: a system can preserve formal exit options while structurally extinguishing them through cost imposition (Reichsfluchtsteuer), administrative binding (hukou), or risk asymmetry (Balseros). The contribution is to formalize these modalities as conditions on system viability rather than as preferences of individual agents."*

### 5.3 "Where is the empirical side?" attack
**BioSystems form**: R2 asked for empirical correlates (R2 was satisfied by the finite model + Weckström forward-ref).
**Likely SRBS form**: "Your historical cases are illustrative but not empirically tested."
**B1 pre-emption**: the "future work" section can point to Paper 2 (AI & Society) where contemporary examples (DAOs, platforms, digital lock-in) will be treated. No need for statistical empirical work for SRBS, which accepts theorisation.

### 5.4 "AI disclosure / authorship" attack
**BioSystems form**: R1.c — declare AI tools.
**Likely SRBS form**: identical Wiley policy.
**B1 pre-emption**: AI use declaration **in the cover letter** + dedicated section in the manuscript before the references (same wording as BioSystems v0.4.13):
> *"AI tools (Claude Opus, GPT, Gemini) were used for language editing, organization of revision materials, calculations check, and clarity checks; final wording, argumentation, and decisions are mine. The author takes full responsibility for the content."*

## 6. Anti-patterns — what NOT to do

| Temptation | Why it is risky |
|---|---|
| Refer to BioSystems more than 3× in B1 | Gives the impression that B1 depends on BioSystems to validate its contribution |
| Quote R3 verbatim in the manuscript | Self-promotion; SRBS reviewers detest it |
| Suggest the same reviewers as BioSystems | Limits the independent pool; SRBS editors will detect it |
| Import the K(S,E) / σ-algebra / categoricity machinery into B1 | Loses the benefit of the derivation; B1 must be readable without BioSystems |
| Submit B1 and another paper of the programme in parallel | Salami slicing — exactly what the transparency ¶3 prevents |
| Hide the research programme in the cover letter | If discovered later, costs trust; declaring it openly is *protective*, not risky |
| Cite Aly 2007 without caution | His "Hitlers Volksstaat" thesis is contested by Tooze; cite for magnitudes only, not for interpretation |

---

## 7. Executive synthesis — 5 concrete levers

1. **Cover letter ¶3 (transparency)** — triptych declaration + forthcoming BioSystems citation = anti-salami-slicing shield.
2. **Cover letter ¶4 (validation)** — R3 verdict + immediate defusal = quality signal without dependence.
3. **B1 bibliography** — import Maturana & Varela 1972, Hofmeyr 2018, Koestler 1967, Ehresmann & Vanbremeersch 2007; leave the rest in BioSystems.
4. **Hirschman pre-emption** — cite Hoffmann 2010 explicitly + clarify the relational vs disjoint delta.
5. **Metaphor abuse pre-emption** — §1 note ("the argument does not depend on a literal isomorphism").

These 5 levers all rest on elements **already present** in the BioSystems material — no additional creative work required, just careful translation.
