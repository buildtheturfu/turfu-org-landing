## Resolution of the 3 locks

### Lock 1 — Explicit finite model

**Problem**: The v3 paper defines an abstract sketch ThP but never constructs a concrete verifiable model. A reviewer can legitimately doubt that the sketch admits a non-trivial model.

**Solution**: Explicit 16-element finite model constructed in Set. Major conceptual change: ev-based φ (current h, not fixed f). The model is computable by hand by any reader in 10 minutes.

**Validation**: Peter G: "The model is no longer abstract, it is computable by hand." This model now anchors the entire paper.

### Lock 2 — E-relative endogeneity

**Problem**: In the category Set, any function factors through any sufficiently large set. The endogeneity condition cannot be a pure categorical invariant — it depends on the structure of E.

**Solution**: Introduction of K(S,E) = {k : S → E | k = κ ∘ π_A for some κ : A → E}. Endogeneity is henceforth a property relative to the environment, not an absolute invariant.

**Validation**: Peter G: "The formulation is epistemologically honest. We no longer claim to have a purely categorical invariant."

### Lock 3 — Admissibility & Criterion 2

**Problem**: Proposition 2 is too close to a definitional reconditioning to deserve the status of a proposition. The extensions (cancer, Luhmann) are speculative and weaken the paper.

**Solution**: Option A adopted: conditions (i)+(ii) only. Proposition 2 downgraded to Criterion 2 (diagnostic criterion). Extensions moved out of the main body.

**Validation**: Peter G: "A criterion that is not a proposition remains a useful tool — and it is more honest than presenting it as a result."

## The strategic problem: v4 too different for an R&R

The v4 is intellectually superior — but structurally unrecognisable compared to the submitted v3. New §2, new §4, Prop 2 → Criterion 2, definitions removed, abstract reformulated. A reviewer would not recognise "their" paper. Consensus of 4 agents on 25 March 2026.

### The diagnosis (Peter G)

The real criterion is not "how many changes", but: **does the reviewer still recognise their object of evaluation?** If so, substantial reinforcements often pass. If not, they may think: "I have been asked to re-review a new manuscript."
