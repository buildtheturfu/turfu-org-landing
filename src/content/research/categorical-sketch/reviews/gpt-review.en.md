## Explication

The paper attempts to construct a **minimal categorical grammar of the viability** of self-organising systems. Rather than talking separately about cells, ecosystems, or social systems, one defines a common abstract structure (ThP), then shows that certain domains are models of it.

Formally: a **P-system** (S, ∂S, φ, ρ) in a symmetric monoidal category, with three constraints — **endogeneity** (φ does not factor through E), **permeability** (the flux passes through the membrane), **membrane stability** (∂S stable under φ).

The most original idea: distinguishing **distributed predation** (viable: competition + exit exist) from **monopolistic predation** (pathological: total capture with no exit).

---

## Four major weaknesses

### 1. Endogeneity in Set — the most important

In the category **Set**, "any function factors through any sufficiently large set". The strength of the condition therefore depends on E being interpreted semantically as "the environment not containing the organisational information".

The proof is not purely structural — it is **proto-formal** or **semi-formal**. The paper acknowledges this clearly and proposes Markov categories / causal kernels as the logical next step.

### 2. Typing of φ — technical problem

The composite β∘Φ∘f has type **A → B**, not **S → S**. The identification with a global endomorphism on S (defined as "union of A, B, Hom(A,B)") is conceptually understandable but **not yet formally properly explicated**.

A missing intermediate construction is needed to encode the whole cycle as an endomorphism on a single object S. *Point to correct.*

### 3. Proposition 2 — definitional reconditioning

A multi-level system is P-compatible iff its inclusions are admissible — "*if and only if by construction*". The real added value lies not in the demonstrative force but in the **shift of the locus of analysis**: from objects to inclusion morphisms.

### 4. Distributed/monopolistic predation — stipulative

A seductive distinction but currently **stipulative**. No strong demonstration yet that this is the correct formalisation of cancer, nor any theorem linking this distinction to standard results in ecological stability. More of a **good working concept** than an established result.
