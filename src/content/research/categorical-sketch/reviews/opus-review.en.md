## Explication of the paper

Christopher Keo's essay proposes a transdisciplinary argument in four stages. The starting point is a categorical formalism called ThP, which mathematically defines the conditions under which a complex multi-level cooperative system remains viable. A "P-system" is viable when its self-production function (φ) remains endogenous — that is, the system produces itself through its own operations, without being captured by an external agent — and when the inclusions between levels (cell → organism, individual → society) preserve this autonomy.

The characteristic failure identified is **monopolistic predation**: an interaction in which a subsystem is subjected to a predator with no possible competition and no possibility of exit. By contrast, **distributed predation** — where competition and exit exist — is compatible with viability. The strong thesis of the paper is that "life is distributed predation", not the absence of predation.

The essay then instantiates this formal condition at three independent levels of reality: **cancer** (deactivation of apoptosis = suppression of the cellular exit morphism), **totalitarianism** (Berlin Wall, confiscation of passports = suppression of political exit), and **ecological invasion in a captive habitat** (invasive predator on an island without refuge). These three cases are not presented as analogies but as independent instantiations of the same formal invariant.

From there, Keo draws two structural principles: **non-heteronomisation** and **the necessity of an admissible exit**. He shows that these principles strongly converge with a libertarian grammar (NAP, private property, right of secession), while acknowledging that the bridge is not yet a closed deduction. This is what he calls "ontological antifascism": not combating fascism as an ideological content, but eliminating its structural condition of possibility by guaranteeing exit.

## Evaluation of relevance and epistemic robustness

### What is solid

The paper does a remarkable job of **epistemic transparency**. The "methodological note" at the opening distinguishes four levels of certainty — proven in ThP, instantiated in the biological, proposed for the political, programmatic for the normative — which is rare in transdisciplinary essays. This intellectual honesty is itself a marker of rigour.

The identification of monopolistic predation as a structural invariant is the **most powerful idea** of the text. It does not rest on an impressionistic analogy between cancer and totalitarianism, but on the independent satisfaction of identical formal conditions at different levels. The recourse to the literature on the major transitions in evolution (Aktipis, Trigos, Michod, Maynard Smith & Szathmáry) anchors the biological argument in a robust empirical corpus.

The distinction between distributed and monopolistic predation — where the discriminating criterion is not the existence of predation but the presence or absence of the third (competition + exit) — is an **authentic conceptual contribution** that goes beyond a mere reformulation of Hirschman.

### What is fragile or under-determined

The most delicate knot is the **passage from the formal to the normative**. The convergence between non-heteronomisation and NAP rests on "additional normative assumptions not yet formalised". This is the declared open front, but it is also the heart of the political thesis.

The political instantiation is **the most fragile of the three**. A "rigorous category of social agents" is not yet constructed. Yet it is precisely this category that would allow formal verification of whether inter-level inclusions in political systems satisfy the same conditions as in the biological.

There is a **risk of partial circularity** in the normative argument: viability conditions are defined in such a way that exit is a necessary component, and then exit is presented as a deduction from the viability conditions. Logically correct, but the normative charge depends entirely on the choice of ThP viability as the structuring value. **Meta-normative question evaded**: why would ThP viability be the relevant criterion for evaluating political systems?

## Scope and implications at various levels

### Epistemological and methodological

The paper proposes a model of operational transdisciplinarity that goes beyond the mere juxtaposition of metaphors. The idea of formalising a structural invariant and then testing it independently at different levels of reality would offer a rigorous bridge between theoretical biology, political theory and ecology — three domains that usually speak to each other through loose analogy. Closer to Rosen's programme than to the "cosmetic mathematisation" frequent in the social sciences.

### Political philosophy

The most radical implication: a shift of the fundamental question — no longer "what is the right political content?" but "what political architecture is viable?". A change of logical level that makes the left/right debate secondary to the structural question of exit. An intellectually powerful position, but one that will be perceived as an attempt to depoliticise the political. The tension between this depoliticising gesture and the avowed convergence with libertarianism is the **principal rhetorical vulnerability**.

### Systems theory and theoretical biology

The most enduring contribution might be the formalisation of the distributed / monopolistic predation distinction. If the sketch ThP is published and validated, this distinction would offer an analytic tool for evaluating the health of any cooperative multi-level system. The reading of cancer as suppression of the exit morphism (rather than mere proliferation) is a reformulation that could influence theoretical biology of cancer (Aktipis/Trigos lineage).

### Digital governance and institutional design

The most immediate potential impact. Two operational questions for any governance protocol: "do the inter-level inclusions preserve endogeneity?" and "does an effective exit exist?". Directly applicable to the design of DAOs, decentralised protocols and digital platforms. The concept of "digital Zomia" (exit guaranteed by the protocol rather than by geography) is a fertile synthesis between Scott, Hirschman and blockchain engineering.

## Synthesis

> This is an ambitious theoretical paper, intellectually stimulating, and remarkably honest about its own limits. Its formal core (ThP sketch + distributed/monopolistic predation distinction) is an **authentic contribution**. Its extension to the political is a **structured conjecture of high quality**, not yet a demonstration. Its principal strength is to pose a question that almost no one poses (what is the formal condition of possibility of totalitarianism?) and to bring to it a coherent transdisciplinary answer. Its principal weakness is that the bridge between structural diagnosis and normative prescription remains a **research programme** rather than an acquired result.
