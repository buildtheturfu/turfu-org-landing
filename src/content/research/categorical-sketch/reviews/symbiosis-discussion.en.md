## 3. Peter G (audit)

### Diagnosis

Hormesis and antifragility retained as directly useful meta-vocabulary. Symbiotic taxonomy deliberately left in the background — compatible with ThP but not necessary for the central demonstration. **Good editorial choice**: avoids opening a second paper.

### Insight: parasitism is NOT coextensive with monopolistic predation

Parasitism itself divides between tolerable distributed forms and pathological monopolistic forms. A real theoretical gain for a future paper.

### The non-sacrificial / ternary approach

Classical biology treats parasitism as a **binary** relation: parasite wins, host loses. The standard response is itself binary: eliminate the parasite (chemotherapy, eradication, repression). This is classical antifascism.

ThP proposes a **ternary** reading: parasitism is not pathological in itself. What is pathological is parasitism *without a third term* — without competition, without exit, without alternative. We do not say "let us eliminate the parasite" (sacrificial, binary), we say "**let us guarantee the third**" (structural, ternary).

This is **Lupasco in action**: neither eradication (homogenisation) nor submission (heterogenisation) but **dynamic equilibrium through the third** (T). And it is exactly what Santacreu was looking for via Proudhon without having the formalism to say it.

## 4. Claude Opus 4 — proposal for B1 §3

### Proposed paragraph for B1 integration (8 lines, end of §3)

> The distinction between distributed and monopolistic predation maps onto the classical ecological spectrum of interspecific interactions. Mutualism corresponds to admissible morphisms; commensalism to weakly non-admissible interactions tolerated by the system; and parasitism divides into distributed forms (compatible with viability when competition and exit exist) and monopolistic forms (incompatible, when both are suppressed). Crucially, the viability framework does not prescribe the elimination of the parasite — a sacrificial, binary response — but the structural guarantee of the third: competition and exit. A system that maintains exit tolerates parasitism without pathology, just as a functioning immune system coexists with parasites without collapse. The response to structural pathology is not eradication but architectural restoration of the exit morphism.
